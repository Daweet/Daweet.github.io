In my undergraduate I studied physics, as I was fascinitated by it --specially by quantum--I decided to pursue my masters degree in quantum physics during which I investigated the statistical properties associated with three-level laser driven by coherent light and coupled to a squeezed vacuum reservoir check this. Squeezed coherent states are very interesting feature squeezed-light. Loosely speaking we say a quantum state is squeezed, in its quadrature for instance, if any of its quadratures has a standard deviation that falls below value of 1/2. This means that in order to fullfill Heisneberg's uncertainity, the uncertainty in one quadrature maybe squeezed below 1, by stretching the uncertainty in the other quadrature.

During my PhD, I studied theory of logic in nano-devices in which I explored and showed the possibilty of using atoms and/or molecules performing complicated computations. Our group showed how dynamics of an atomic system, which upon perturbation by external field, is able to mimic logic computations, Boolean and Non-Boolean. The physical system can be solid or solution, and the perturbation can be optical or electrical. In both cases the dynamics can be explored using Lie-algebraic approach in which the physical parameters are embeded as generators.
